What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a technology that enables computers to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, and decision-making.

AI is not magic‚Äîit's mathematics, statistics, and computer science working together. Think of AI as an extremely capable assistant that can help you with repetitive, time-consuming tasks so you can focus on creative and meaningful work.

Why Should You Learn About AI?

  • Empowerment: Understanding AI helps you use it confidently and effectively
  • Career Growth: AI skills are increasingly valuable across all industries
  • Critical Thinking: Know when to trust AI and when to question it
  • Innovation: Use AI to solve real-world problems in your field

üí° Important: AI is a tool designed to augment human capabilities, not replace them. It handles repetitive and data-intensive tasks, freeing humans to focus on creativity, empathy, and complex decision-making.

How Does AI Work?

Machine learning models learn patterns from data

AI systems learn from data through a process called machine learning. Instead of being explicitly programmed for every scenario, AI models identify patterns and make predictions based on examples.

The AI Learning Process:

  1. Data Collection: Gather relevant information and examples
  2. Training: The AI model learns patterns from the data
  3. Testing: Evaluate the model's accuracy and performance
  4. Deployment: Use the trained model to make predictions
  5. Refinement: Continuously improve based on new data

Types of AI You Might Encounter:

  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): ChatGPT, translation tools, voice assistants
  • Computer Vision: Facial recognition, medical imaging, self-driving cars
  • Recommendation Systems: Netflix suggestions, online shopping recommendations
  • Predictive Analytics: Weather forecasting, financial predictions

The official U.S. government framework for managing AI risks, developed by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). Published January 2023.

üîó Official framework recognized by U.S. government agencies, international organizations, and Fortune 500 companies. Learn more at NIST.gov

1. Govern

Establish AI Governance

Create organizational structure and policies for responsible AI use.

  • üîπ Know your organization's AI policy - Check if your employer/school has AI usage guidelines
  • üîπ Understand who's accountable - Know who reviews AI-generated work in your context
  • üîπ Set personal AI boundaries - Decide what you will/won't use AI for
  • üîπ Document AI usage - Keep track of when and how you use AI tools
Example: "I will not use AI to complete graded assignments without permission, but I can use it to brainstorm ideas and check grammar."
üó∫Ô∏è

2. Map

Understand AI Context & Risks

Identify potential risks and understand how AI systems work.

  • üîπ Know the AI's purpose - Is it trained for your specific use case?
  • üîπ Identify personal risks - What could go wrong if AI makes a mistake?
  • üîπ Understand data flow - Where does your input go? Who can see it?
  • üîπ Consider stakeholders - Who is affected by AI-generated decisions?
Example: "If I use AI to help write a resume, the risk is inaccurate information. If I use it for medical advice, the risk could be life-threatening‚Äîbetter to consult a doctor."
üìä

3. Measure

Assess & Benchmark AI Risks

Evaluate AI reliability and trustworthiness for your specific needs.

  • üîπ Test AI outputs - Verify accuracy with multiple sources
  • üîπ Check for bias - Does the AI give fair responses across different scenarios?
  • üîπ Assess consistency - Does the same question get similar answers?
  • üîπ Evaluate appropriateness - Is this AI tool suitable for your task?
Example: "I asked the AI the same coding question 3 times and got different answers. This tells me I should verify the code independently before using it."
üéØ

4. Manage

Respond to AI Risks

Prioritize and address identified risks proactively.

  • üîπ Prioritize high-risk tasks - Use human review for critical decisions
  • üîπ Have backup plans - Don't rely solely on AI for important work
  • üîπ Report problems - Flag inaccurate, biased, or harmful outputs
  • üîπ Continuous learning - Stay updated on AI safety practices
Example: "For my job application, I'll use AI to draft the cover letter, but I'll have a career counselor review it before submitting‚Äîtoo important to trust AI alone."

How to Protect Your Identity When Using AI

Treat AI like a public space.

  • Never type exact personal details - Avoid names, addresses, phone numbers, or specific dates
  • Replace specifics with general descriptions - Use "a manager at a large company" instead of "my boss at Amazon"
  • Assume every prompt creates a digital footprint - Think before you type

Example:

‚ùå "My address is 123 Main St, help me find nearby clinics"

‚úÖ "Assume a random suburban location, what clinics might be available?"

üí° Key Takeaway: Use AI without exposing yourself. This is AI literacy.

üîí

AI Tools That Don't Use Your Data

Not all AI tools train on your data.

  • Some AI models offer privacy mode - Look for "no-training" or "private mode" options
  • Check data policies before typing - Read the privacy statement
  • Choose tools with strict data policies - Examples include Anthropic's Claude, local models, and privacy-focused alternatives

Questions to Ask:

‚úÖ "Does this tool train AI models on my conversations?"

‚úÖ "Is there a privacy mode or business/pro tier with better data protection?"

‚úÖ "How long is my data retained?"

üí° Key Takeaway: Choose tools that respect your privacy.

üí¨

How to Ask AI Questions Without Leaking Identity

You can ask AI anything ‚Äî without exposing yourself.

  • Use hypothetical prompts - Frame questions as "imagine..." or "assume..."
  • Replace real details with examples - Use generic names like "Company X" or "City Y"
  • Focus on the task, not your identity - Get accurate answers without personal context

Prompt Transformations:

‚ùå "Help me write a letter to my boss John at Amazon's Seattle office about my salary negotiation"

‚úÖ "Help me write a professional letter to a manager at a large tech company about salary negotiations"


‚ùå "I live in Miami and work in healthcare, what career paths should I consider?"

‚úÖ "For someone in healthcare in a major US city, what career advancement options exist?"

üí° Key Takeaway: Stay safe and smart with AI by keeping prompts general.

Reduce Your AI Digital Footprint

Every interaction with AI can leave traces.

  • Avoid combining identifiable details - Even general info can be linked together
  • Use different AI tools for different tasks - Don't centralize all your queries in one place
  • Regularly review and delete chat history - Most AI platforms allow you to clear conversations
  • Consider using privacy-focused browsers - Or incognito mode when using AI tools

Digital Footprint Reduction Tips:

‚úÖ Clear your AI chat history monthly

‚úÖ Use different AI tools for work vs. personal queries

‚úÖ Never mention multiple identifying details in one conversation

‚úÖ Review account settings for data sharing options

üí° Key Takeaway: Minimize your digital footprint for long-term privacy.
